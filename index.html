Five Structural Shifts Reshaping GSIs

1. Pricing Model Collapse

GenAI can reduce an 8-hour coding task to 2 hours, making hourly billing untenable. GSIs are pivoting to outcome-based and subscription pricing. PwC One (launched March 2026) uses consumption-based pricing. This changes how technology partners get embedded in deals.

2. New Practice Areas Emerging

Data preparation for AI ($400B market in 2024, projected $2.2T by 2034), AI governance, responsible AI, and agentic AI orchestration are all net-new practice areas. Identity is foundational infrastructure for all of them.

3. Pyramid Model Under Pressure

AI tools can perform 80% of a junior analyst’s research and slide generation. Starting salaries frozen at top consultancies. The traditional leverage model is being replaced by smaller teams with AI augmentation. Technology partnerships matter more than headcount.

4. AI-First Delivery Platforms

Every major GSI now has an internal AI platform: McKinsey’s Lilli, PwC’s PwC One, EY’s EYQ, Deloitte’s Ascend/Zora. These platforms are becoming client-facing — creating new partnership surfaces for technology vendors.

Every GSI is building dedicated AI-cybersecurity practices. Accenture launched Cyber.AI with Anthropic. Deloitte published Cyber AI Blueprints for agentic AI. Capgemini declared cybersecurity must become “identity-first in an AI-first world.” This is the exact whitespace for Thales.

6. Headcount Divergence

Deloitte: +10K net. Accenture: restructured (laid off 11K, hired 77K AI/data). PwC: -5,600. The firms growing are growing in AI. The firms shrinking are shedding traditional roles. Where you sit in a GSI’s AI stack determines your relevance.
