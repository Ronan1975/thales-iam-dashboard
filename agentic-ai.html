1. HSM-Backed Agent Identity

Thales Luna HSMs provide hardware root of trust for agent credentials. Every competitor uses software-only key storage. In a world where agent credentials can be exfiltrated (M&S proved this), HSM backing is the ultimate differentiator. Thales Luna HSM; FIPS 140-3

2. EU Data Sovereignty

EU-hosted, French defence company parent (€22.1B). Appeals to EU institutions, government, regulated industries. No US vendor can make this claim. With Digital Sovereignty Act + Gaia-X, this matters more every quarter. Thales Annual Report; EU DSA

3. GDPR-Native Consent for AI Agents

Standalone consent management module — no competitor offers consent as infrastructure for agent-to-human interactions. When an AI agent processes customer data, who gave consent? Under what scope? With what right of withdrawal? Only OneWelcome answers this. GDPR Art. 7, 22; OW Consent Module

4. Agent Assurance Levels

Like LOA (Levels of Assurance) for humans but for AI agents. Define trust tiers: Level 1 (sandbox), Level 2 (limited scope), Level 3 (full autonomous). Map to NIST and eIDAS trust frameworks. Nobody has defined this yet. First to standardize wins. NIST SP 800-63; eIDAS LOA

5. Consumer-Delegated Agent Authority

“This AI agent acts on behalf of [Customer] with permissions [X, Y, Z] until [date].” Delegated authority for consumer-facing agents with consent, scope limits, and auto-expiry. The CIAM problem nobody else is solving. OAuth 2.0 RAR; OW delegation spec

6. Cross-Border Federated Agent Identity

Agents operating across EU member states need federated identity that works with EUDI Wallet and eIDAS 2.0. OneWelcome already handles 75% of Dutch eHerkenning and is eIDAS-certified. Natural extension to agent identity federation. eIDAS 2.0; eHerkenning
